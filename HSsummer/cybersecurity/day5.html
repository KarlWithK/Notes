Direct Attacks on Computational Devices

Environmental Attacks

  • Electricity: needs energy 24/7
  • Temperate: CPU are hot so they need the right temperature
  • Limited conductance: Make sure there is no electric magnetic force in the room

Eavesdropping

Secretly listen to someone's conversation.

  • The direct observation techniques are commonly referred to as should surfings.

Wiretapping

  • Use Inexpensive coaxial copper cables -> information is transmitted via electrical impulses in these cables.
  • Relatively inexpensive means that attackers can reconstruct the data being transferred through the tapped cable and eavesdrop on network traffic.
  • Wiretapping attacks are passive and very difficult to detect

Signal Eminations

  • Computers screens emit radio frequencies that can be used to detect what is being displayed.
  • Visible light reflections can also be used to reconstruct a display from its reflection on a wall, coffee mug, or eyeglass.

Acoustic Emissions

  • An attacker could use an audio recording of a user typing on a keyboard to reconstruct what was typed since each keystrokes has minute difference in the sound it produces, and curtain keys are known to be pressed more than others.
  • A trained neural network recognized 79% of all keystrokes.

Hardware key loggers

A key logger is any means of recording a victim's keystrokes, typically used to eavesdrop passwords or other sensitive information.

  • Typically small connectors that are installed between keyboard and a computer.

TEMPEST

US government code word for a set of standards for limiting information-carrying electromagnetic emanations from computing equipment. Thee zones that determine protection level:

  1. No direct contact with equipment. 1 Meter
  2. No closer than 20 meters or some sort of blockage.
  3. No closer than 100 meters

Emanation Blockage

  • We can enclose sensitive equipment in a windowless roomm
  • To block acoustic emanations, we use sound-dampening materials.
  • To block electromagnetic emanations we can make sure every cord and cable is well grounded and insulated:
    • Faraday cage is a perfect example of this. They have holes that smaller than the wavelengths of the electromagnetic radiation and block it from passing.

Computer Forensics

Practice of obtaining information contained on a electronic devices for legal proceedings.

  • Attacks use same techniques.
  • Involves the physical inspection of the components of a computer to the microscopic level or the electronic inspection of a computer's parts.

ATMS

An automatic teller machine (ATM) is any device that allows customers of financial institutions to complete withdrawn and deposit transactions without humans assistance.

  • Has internal cryptographic processor that encrypts the entered pin and compares it to cache
  • The processor encrypts all information to ensure confidentiality of a user.
  • Runs under the Triple DES (3DES) crytosystem standard, a legacy symmetric cryposystem with up to 112 bits of security.
  • 3DES secret keys installed on an ATM are either loaded on-site by technicians or downloaded remotely from the vender

Attacks on ATMs

  • Lebanese Loop: A sleeve is inserted into the card slot that makes the user card be stuck in the sleeve.
  • Skimmer: a device that stores users' credit information to make duplicates of the original card
  • Fake ATMs: capture all information.
