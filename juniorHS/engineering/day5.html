  1. Generation is only the first of many steps our power takes on its nearly instantaneous journey form production to consumption.
  2. The electricity normally makes its way through a series of discrete steps: generation ( production of electricity ), transmission( moving electricity to populated arenas ), and Distribution.
  3. Substations are the linkages that connect the whole system together. Short loads
  4. Substations is now a general term for a facility that can serve a wide variety of critical roles on the power grid. Depends on what the goal is. General is switching.
  5. As a junction point the grid, a substations often serves as the termination of many individual power lines. Creates redundancy and complexity: connection to these various devices are called buses
  6. At high voltages, even air can act like a conductor, which means even if you create break in a line, electricity can continue flowing in a phenomenon known as an arc. Defeats the purpose of the switch
  7. A fault: something that causes a short circuit on the power line. Common: short circuit to ground.
  8. Fuse, a one time item that prevents s short circuit. Circuit breaker, that as dielectric that stops the flow of electricity. Sometimes in a tank with non-conductive oil or dense dielectric gas.
  9. A special kind of circuit breaker called a recloser can interrupt current for a short period of time and re-energize the line to test if the fault has cleared.
  10. Transformers allow the voltage of the freed to be brought back down before distribution. A device that relies on the alternating current of the grid to function. The induced voltage will be proportional to the ratio of loops
  11. Instrument transformers are small transformers used to measure the voltage or current on grid or provide power to system monitoring devices.
  12. Regulators are devices with multiple taps that can make small adjustments - up or down to the distribution line on the feeder line
  13. Safety and are built with a grid of grounding rods and conduction buried below the surface. Same voltages level, an equipotential.
