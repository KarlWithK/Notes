What are PCBs? // How do PCBs Work? https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z2LgmIGE2nI

  • PCB stands for the printed circuit board and the circuit we actually see is really a multi layered labyrinth of hundreds of copper wires.
  • Microchips, Resistors, capacitors, and connectors are solder into the PCB - the motherboard. The PCB is just a flat board without anything on it.
  • The System On a Chip, or SoC for short, is mounted on a ball grid array and connects the brain of the smart phone to the wires or traces that run the PCB.
  • The PCB allows each of the components to communicate with microchips via hundreds of wires that stretch to the size of a football field in rather precise organization.
  • PCB are made of multiple conductive layers of copper: top and bottom layers are used for mounting components and acting as multiple and antennae, and some layers are for power plane and ground plane and signal tracing.
  • Each of the conductive layers is composed of copper and between each of the layers in an insulating fiberglass and epoxy resin that prevents flow of electricity. Most PCBs have 2 to 10 layers.
  • VIAS, or vertical interconnect accesses perform this function. Vias are drilled and metal plated holes that connect two or more layers. Three types: through holes, blind via, and buried via.
  • Some PCBS are split into a motherboard (bigger one) and a daughter board (small one), some are stacked on top of each other to fit more space for a larger battery. Phones PCBS are light and small weighing 14.2g.
  • Through hole components are components that mounted a leg going thorough a hole. They are big, while the ones we have now are called SMDs, or surface Mount Device.
