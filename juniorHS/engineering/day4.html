Understanding Poisson's Ratio https://youtu.be/tuOlM3P7ygA

  1. The ratio gives us an understanding about how different materials deform under loading
  2. Perpendicular is the lateral axis while the direction the load is applied is longitudinal axis. The material will deform in the lateral direction
  3. Possion's ration tell us how much the material will deform in the lateral directions.
  4. When the longitudinal load is applied the strains in the lateral directions are equal and proportional to the strain in the longitudinal direction.
  5. The ratio between the longitudinal strain and the lateral strain is a material constant, nu and is called Possion's ratio named after Simeon Denis Possion. It's positive. Only applied to isotropic materials - materials that have the same properties in the same direction
  6. The theoretical range of possible values for Possion's ratio is goes from -1 to 0.5, but in practice it's more like 0 to 0.5 and metals 0.3
  7. When a material that has a ratio with a value of zero, there will be no deformation in the lateral direction.
  8. Materials with a negative ratio are known as auxetic materials and they expand laterally when pulled and contract when compressed.
  9. We can use Hooke's law in combination with Possion's ratio, and the principle of superposition, obtain the equation for strain in all directions (1 / E)[οdirection - υ(οdirection + οdirection)]. Known as the generalized Hooke's law
  10. Materials with a ratio of 0.5 will have their volumetric strain equal to zero, meaning that materials with 0.5's volume will remain constant as it deforms. Known as incompressible materials.
