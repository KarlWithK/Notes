What is biomedical engineering? https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KQm-gfobUm8

  1. It's about using the ideas of engineering to make equipment, devices, and medicine to focus on the human body.
  2. Artificial limbs are one of the things that biomedical engineers have to make that are popular and they have to deign it with the fact that it will move.
  3. The electrical engineering parts comes from the fact that there has to be signals to tell the body parts to move.
  4. A biomedical engineer tests how different biomaterial affects the body and how it can be used to help humans.
  5. Another part of biomedical engineering is to 3D print organs and other technological devices such as defibrillators or pacemakers to save lives.
  6. It is very broad so you don't actually focus on everything, instead you can go into specific like bioinstrumentation and programming limbs to move based on need. There are more to choose from.
  7. It might be best to major in mechanical or electrical engineering and then get a master's in biomedical engineering. This is so everything trans out cheaper and you get more opportunities.
