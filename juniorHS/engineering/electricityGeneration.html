How electricity generation really works: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AHFZVn38dTM&list=PLTZM4MrZKfW-ftqKGSbO-DwDiOGqNmq53&index=2

  1. Generation is the first step electricity takes on its journey through the power grid.
  2. We can't really store electricity so we have to generate it, transport and supply it at the same time.
  3. We generate most of our electricity by converting heat or steam thermal.
  4. Many plants use fossil fuel and make carbon dioxide and some use fission of radioactive elements. Some use geothermal energy.
  5. Power stations are pretty much the same: once the steam is created, it passes through a turbine which converts the thermal energy into rotational energy.
  6. The turbine has these parts: has a rotor that spins a set of magnets. The stator (parts that's stationary). These items work together and creates a voltage
  7. Three individual phases is used to get a a smooth phase that is never zero. This allows for a stable power grid.
  8. Also can use hydroelectricity and solar photovoltaic (pv)but there are some cost: no inertia ( power to remain in motion on the grid ) and only works when the sun is out.
  9. Inertia comes from the physical rotation of all those massive interconnected generators. Allows the energy to keep on following.
  10. Load following is when generation is constantly adjusted up or down to avoid fluctuations and to match demand.
  11. Synchroscope helps getting the frequency just right and to connect everything
