How Rwanda built a drone delivery service https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jEbRVNxL44c

  1. In the developing world, drones are used to deliver medical supplies in Rwanda. Because they have a lack of roads.
  2. Each drone is capable of carrying 1.6 kg of medical supplies, 3 500 milliliter blood bags.
  3. The design was based on: the speed the plane needs to get into the air (averaging 5 minutes from order to launch), needs a gps collection and its energy used - that's why they went with a plane design. It's made light
  4. to remove the delay from the gps they moved it to the battery to always have it on and always connected. Removed 10-15 minutes. And made it a modular design.
  5. Their pre-flight test is done by checking the flight surfaces through a mobile app that connects to the launcher system.
  6. They have a pulley and an electric motor to launch the drone in 0.3 seconds. It launches the same way every time and reliably clears the obstacles in front of it.
  7. Since take off and landing are the most difficult stages, so to avoid landing they drop the package. To land they have a system that "catches" the drone by the hook at its end.
  8. The drone has two of everything to make everything safe, that's why they got rid of the old landing system - it wasn't safe. No injuries so far.
  9. The drones cruise at 100 km/h with a range of 160 kms, and serve an 80km zone, weights just 6.4 kilograms, made from carbon fibre composite. Batteries is the heaviest - 10 kg, but it is rated at 1.25 kWh
  10. Fitting a large autonomous network of drone into the already busy and highly regulated american airspace would be difficult, they are the testers
  11. Makes 1 GB per hour of flight. And more data could help them improve so much more, and are growing more.
  12. It could be used for all kinda of disasters. Like viruses.
