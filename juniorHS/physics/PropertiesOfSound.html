Sound

  • Sound has the fundamental properties of waves.
  • Amplitude = sound
  • Sound is a vibration that travels as a wave though solids, liquids or gases.
  • Anything that vibrates makes sound. Can't see that sound is a wave because the frequency is too high to see the individual vibrations.
  • 20 Hz we can hear, but we prefer the 100Hz and 2,000 Hz
  • Sound has properties of loudness and pitch -> loudness depends on the amplitude of the vibration. -> A speaker making a loud sound moves back and forth more than a speaker making a soft sound. -> The pitch of a sound depends on the frequency of vibration.
  • Sounds travel faster in liquids and solids than they do in air.
  • Sounds cannot travel through the vacuum of space. Sound waves can only move through matter
  • The frequency of sound tells you how fast the pressure oscillates.
  • A low frequency sound has a low pitch while a high frequency has a high pitch.
  • Most sounds we hear contain not just one but many frequencies.
  • Superposition principle -> complex sound is created by the superposition of many frequencies.
  • Our ears' nerves respond separately to each different frequency.
  • Sonic image.
  • The loudness of sound is measured in decibels (dB) which is determined mostly by the amplitude of a sound wave.
  • How we hear sound is affected by the frequency of the sound + amplitude.
  • Human voice is in the larynx
  • A speaker is a device that is specially designed to reproduce sound accurately.
  • Acoustics is the science and technology of sound.
  • Microphone transforms a sound wave into an electrical signal with the same pattern of oscillation
  • Digital to analog converter
  • Stereo is actually two recordings one played from the right and the other from the left -> more alive
