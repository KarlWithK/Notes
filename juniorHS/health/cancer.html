Part 2 -- How Stress Affects Us.

A stressor is a challenge that may be positive or negative. Either way, it triggers the stress response. The perception of stress is subjective. Not all people experience stress in the same way or to the same degree. The intensity of stress is sometimes learned from people around us as we observe how they handle challenges in their life. Some types of stress come from our external environment while other types are more internal -- they lie deep within our personality.

Positive stress is called 'eustress'. It is the anticipation, exhilaration and excitement one feels when faced with a positive event such as graduation, getting a driver's license, playing a championship game or taking an overseas trip. Negative stress is known as 'distress'. Distress is triggered by challenges that are overwhelming and may have a negative outcome such as the death of a loved one or a divorce.

Human beings cannot live without some degree of stress. Stress motivates us and acts as the driving force to move forward.

It is very important to place stress into the proper perspective. Sometimes we get too caught up with daily hassles that are not worth our time or energy. Hence, the comments 'Don't sweat the small stuff' or 'Roll with the punches'.
