America has always been called a melting pot — a land that blends all kinds of cultures due to its image as the land of immigrants. Primarily, this image was greatly developed through three waves of immigration: the first taking place in the 1840s as Northwestern Europeans came to America and settled down. The second wave was between the 1890 and 1924, where many Southern and Eastern Europeans immigrated as they arrived at Ellis Island.
