Party Eras in American Government:

  • Party Eras and Critical election
  • 1796-1924: first system:
    • Federalist v Democratic Republicans
    • Federalists end after the War of 1812
  • 1828-1856: Second system:
    • Democrats (Jackson and Van Buren V Whigs)
    • 1860-1928: Two Republican Eras:
      • First from 1860, second from 1896
      • End with the great depression
    • 1932-1964: New Deal Coalition:
      • Urbanites, unions, the poor, southerners, blacks
      • Ended by Great Society, Vietnam
    • 1968-now: Southern Realignment, Divided Gov't:
      • The South goes Republican
      • Many presidents sparring with a hostile congress
      • Party realignment
  • Third Parties:
    • They typically come in three flavors:
      • Single issue (Prohibition, Liberation)
      • Splinter (TR's Progessives)
      • Based on charismatic individuals (Perot)
    • Why do parties dominate?
      • The electoral system
        • First past pos, single member districts
    • How could we get more than two?:
      • Proportional Representation, aka Party list.
  • Understanding Parties:
    • Essential elements of democratic government
    • An unanticipated development
    • Parties are often too undisciplined to follow through on their own agendas (Clinton on health case, Trump on ending obamacare)
    • America's weak party system makes it very difficult to pass ambitious and sweeping laws.
    • But given a sprawling and divided America, is this necessarily a bad thing?
