Should we get rid of the two-party system in America? Why or why not, explain!

The United States has survived over two hundred years due to its nature to change. Yet, as Troutman mentioned in his editorial, the Framers were extremely worried whether America would be overtaken by a majority. Even, Gorge Washington warned about the two-party system we have today. Ultimately, however, this system drives competition between the two parties to make America better and gives the opportunity for issues to be heard and addressed.

America's diversity is impressive. It's like a pizza with fifty different toppings that somehow comes together to create something extraordinary. However, included with such a pizza are a wide range of problems: each that must be addressed. The two-party system addresses this diversity by representing certain factions in their policy. Based on the proportion of what most of their members want, the elected president of a party will addresses their political agenda. Therefore, instead of having a multitude of factions that individually create policies that only benefit themselves, the two-party system allows for multiple problems to be addressed. With the past record of presidents addressing 80% of the policies they were elected for, it can be seen that the system is a success.

However, success produced by system is not its main selling point. Instead that award goes to the change that is brought by the competition between the Democratic and Republican parties. Voting for a party can be seen as from an economic viewpoint, where the voters are the consumers and the policies created are the products. With only two "companies" competing for the consumers, they must make their best effort to do the best they can. After all, a vote for the other party can be seen as a lost of revenue their business. This competition assures citizens that parties will always keep advancing in an effort to get more voters and get that bread. In addition, the Constitution's two term limit creates an internal conflict that prevents an obstacle for a never ending status quo.
