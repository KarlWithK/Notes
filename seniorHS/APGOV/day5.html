  • What is federalism?:
    • A nation composed of semiautonomous regions.
    • Compared to unitary governments.
    • States of the USA are unitary with respect to their local regions
      • The city is the creation of the state
    • Confederation:
      • A nation composed of autonomous region
      • They tend to dissolve or merge
    • Why federalism?:
      • Only about 11 nations
      • Useful for big diverse lands
  • The basis of federalism:
    • No real basis in the enlightment-the USA just happened to have 13 powerful political entities
    • States have responsibility to run elections for state and federal jobs.
    • Equal representation in the senate, fixed in place by Article V.
    • Federal government is obliged to protect the states, guarantee "republican government"
    • Overlapping "concurrent" government
  • National supremacy:
    • The supremacy clause (article VI)
    • All states are bound by and must respect the US constitution:
      • Occasional challenges like nullification, weed, etc.
    • The civil war
    • The civil right movement
    • Abortion, women's rights, gay rights
    • But what about the 10th Amendment?:
      • Creates the reserved or "police" powers of states-many debates
    • 11th Amendment: Creates state immunity
  • Implied powers:
    • John Marshall planted seeds
    • McCulloch V Maryland (1819):
      • Could Maryland tax the national bank?
      • Webster's argument is based on the necessary and proper clause.
      • Marhsall aggress and establishes that:
        • The federal government is supreme and preempts states
        • Implied powers are real (v enumerated powers)
  • Commerce Power:
    • Gibbons v Ogden (1824)
    • John Marshall's extremely expansive definition of commerce
    • National government attempts to regulate states during the progressive Era, get shot down
    • New Deals law all struck down
    • UNTIL the "switch in time that saved nine":
      • Wickard V Filburn (1942)
